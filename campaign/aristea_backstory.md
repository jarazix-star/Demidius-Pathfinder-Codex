# Aristea of the Shifting Tides

This record describes Aristea's life and abilities before the Battle for Tradegulf. Her current campaign status remains unchanged: Eris killed her with *death knell* in that battle, concealed her death by making her appear alive but unconscious, and pulled her soul into Tartarus.

## Devotee of Nereus

Aristea was an elven island wizard, sea-devotee, merchant, and shapeshifter. Her worship of Nereus deepened during years of travel across treacherous waters. She sought forgotten reefs, drowned shrines, and tide-locked caverns where ancient offerings had calcified in salt.

Nereus rewarded her patience, wisdom, and devotion through quiet transformation. Aristea's relationship with the sea was not limited to commanding waves; her magic allowed her to become fluid and transformative like the sea itself.

## Arcane disciplines

Aristea blended dwarven runic structure with oceanic mysticism. The runic tradition describes her magical training rather than her ancestry.

Her water magic included tidal manipulation, pressure shaping, current-driven propulsion, suspended-brine bindings, and shaping seawater into blades, veils, and shields. Her cold magic flash-froze summoned water, drew heat into frost fields, encased enemies in ice, and formed glacial sigils that stored and released spell energy. She treated cold as preservation, stillness, and inevitability rather than uncontrolled destruction.

Aristea also mastered controlled shapeshifting. She understood form as something fluid and shaped by purpose. Her transformations were elegant: moonlit scales, ornamental frost, and stable forms rather than grotesque or uncontrolled mutation.

## Animal Lord of the Tides

At a submerged ruin sacred to Nereus, Aristea completed a divine trial by navigating crushing depths without magic and relying only on faith and breath. Through boons accumulated during her pilgrimages, Nereus bestowed the sacred Animal Lord of the Tides template.

The mantle granted Aristea a sacred narwhal form. Its spiraled horn served as an underwater focus for water and cold magic; the form could survive arctic seas, calm lesser marine creatures, and sense magical disturbances through sonar-like perception. Among some island mystics, this earned her the name **the Silver Tusk of Nereus**.

## Merchant-Mage of the Isles

Aristea's voyages combined trade in rare arctic reagents and deep-sea components, recovery of relics from submerged ruins, and quiet missions for Nereus. Sailors told stories of a narwhal guiding ships through fog before an elven mage appeared at the next port.

## The unfinished tide

Aristea and Demidius shared a thoughtful and restrained love. Demidius chose to wait until marriage before sexual intimacy, and Aristea honored that choice. They parted before she received the Animal Lord mantle and before he fully entered his mythic path.

The later campaign record confirms that Aristea became Demidius's cohort and received supernatural Loyalty toward him through the inherited demiplane. It does not yet establish when the two reunited, whether they resumed their romance, or how the earlier relationship interacted with the later supernatural bond.

## Current status

Eris later killed Aristea with *death knell* at the Battle for Tradegulf. Eris made her appear alive but unconscious before pulling her soul into Tartarus. Resurrection cannot succeed until the party locates, reaches, and releases it.
