# Artifact Research Protocol

The party's artifacts are campaign-changing assets whose complete functions should not be assumed from a single detection spell or field inspection.

## Procedure

1. **Secure and isolate the object.** Record its provenance, known activations, apparent school or aura, and any observed cost, curse, intelligence, or allegiance.
2. **Gather its history.** Use witnesses, divine contacts, former owners, inscriptions, and *legend lore*. Historical information may reveal purpose and dangers without revealing complete mechanics.
3. **Use a specialized collection.** Research for approximately one week in a library aligned with the artifact's origin or function, unless the GM establishes another interval.
4. **Make the relevant Knowledge check.** A published default for artifacts commonly starts at DC 15 + the artifact's caster level, with additional information for every 5 points by which the result exceeds the DC. The GM controls the actual DC and revelations.
5. **Test incrementally.** Use controlled trials, layered defenses, observers, and an extraction plan before relying on an incompletely understood ability.
6. **Update the artifact record.** Separate witnessed functions, researched claims, hypotheses, and unresolved risks.

## Library support

A purpose-built library can reasonably provide a circumstance bonus, with published examples typically in the +2 to +5 range. The party should develop specialized collections for planar gates, primordial sea magic, divine relics, and dangerous random artifacts.

## Current applications

- **Key of Daedalus:** prioritize planar routing, access limits, hostile interception, and emergency closure.
- **Scepter of Keto:** prioritize navigation-curse bypass, component interactions, range, and countermeasures.
- **Deck of Many Things:** preserve as an untouched high-risk reserve until its exact deck and safeguards are known.
- **New acquisitions:** do not treat *identify* or *analyze dweomer* as a complete mechanical disclosure.

**Source:** *Artifacts & Legends* (PZO9250), PDF pp. 6–7 / printed pp. 4–5.
