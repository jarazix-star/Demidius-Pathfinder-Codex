# Calculated Luck

**Rating:** A, conditional

**Priority:** Add to Demidius's preparation and spell-access review

**Verification:** Verified against the local *Occult Mysteries* PDF; d20PFSRD independently confirms the spell entry and Section 15 attribution
**Source:** *Pathfinder Campaign Setting: Occult Mysteries* (PZO9269), PDF pp. 52-53 / printed pp. 50-51; [d20PFSRD spell entry](https://www.d20pfsrd.com/magic/all-spells/c/calculated-luck/)

## Why it matters

*Calculated luck* is a personal cleric 3/witch 4 divination lasting 1 round per level. The caster rolls 3d8 and assigns the results among an energy vulnerability, a spell-school caster-level increase, and a specified kind of d20 roll. A result of 6 assigned to the d20 category grants a +2 luck bonus on caster-level checks for the duration.

Under CR-01 and CR-02, that qualifying +2 luck bonus should become +4. Unlike Seven-Pipped Gem, the spell can cover repeated caster-level checks without spending an immediate action on each check. It therefore has potential value before a planned sequence of dispels, spell-resistance checks, or other caster-level contests.

## Limits and opportunity cost

- The desired result is random. At least one 6 appears on 3d8 about 33% of the time.
- The spell also imposes an energy vulnerability determined through its assignment procedure.
- A luck bonus does not stack with a larger luck bonus on the same check. Seven-Pipped Gem remains stronger for a single decisive roll.
- The spell consumes a standard action unless cast before the encounter or accelerated through another legal effect.
- The exact assignment procedure and interaction with reroll effects should not be expanded beyond the printed text without a GM ruling.

## Disposition

Treat *calculated luck* as a verified preparation candidate, not a replacement for Seven-Pipped Gem. Its best role is pre-combat coverage when Demidius expects several caster-level checks and can tolerate the randomly selected vulnerability.
