# Super Genius / Rogue Genius Integration Update

**Codex revision:** v3.4  
**Scope:** Integration of the first ten content-level Advanced Options extractions into Demidius, Dawnrunner, and Pillar recommendations.  
**Verification standard:** Mechanics marked “Verified source” were extracted directly from the uploaded PDFs. Acquisition, stacking, and campaign permission remain separate questions.

## Executive Finding

The new material does not materially replace Demidius’s current core build. Its greatest value is in three areas:

1. **Acquireable hex effects** that improve save debuffing, action denial, anti-caster control, and prepared defenses.
2. **Dawnrunner officer and follower builds** that convert the ship into a coordinated mythic organization.
3. **Infrastructure subsystems**, especially spagyric devices, that can outfit multiple allies for specific missions.

## Priority Decisions

### 1. Determine Demidius’s legal hex-acquisition routes

The most important unresolved rules question is not whether **Doom Gaze**, **Mock**, **Rune Hex**, **Wish Giver**, **Whispers**, or **Dark Desires** are strong. It is whether Demidius can legally obtain each through Spirit Guide, feats, revelations, divine boons, mythic abilities, or campaign-specific power selection. They should remain on the watchlist until each route is verified.

### 2. Add a dedicated Navigator to the Dawnrunner

A high-level Navigator ranger is the cleanest immediate improvement to Dawnrunner logistics. The archetype’s benefits are difficult to reproduce with ordinary gear because they affect voyage time, random encounters, the crew’s initiative and awareness, and ship-specific rerolls.

### 3. Decide whether the campaign permits Spagyric Devices

This is a potentially transformative subsystem and deserves an explicit GM ruling before build resources are committed. If permitted, the ideal user is a dedicated follower, cohort, or quartermaster—not Demidius.

### 4. Build an officer doctrine around teamwork distribution

Ship’s Officer, Crown Privateer, cavalier orders, and Superior Tactician point toward a formal officer layer that distributes morale, sacred defenses, teamwork feats, and voyage bonuses. These stack conceptually with Demidius’s Suzerain role even when specific bonus types do not stack numerically.

## Options Not Recommended for Demidius

- **Star-Crossed curse:** directly undermines the Probability Engine by converting natural 11s into automatic failures.
- **Variable Curse:** +1 oracle caster level is tempting for dispel checks, but random daily curse exposure is strategically unacceptable.
- **Direct cavalier or summoner multiclassing:** the strong options are better placed on officers, followers, or custom allies.
- **Most slayer lethalities:** useful for assassins and martial specialists, but they do not advance Demidius’s core role.
