# Blessed and Champion Progression

Blessed and Champion describe a follower's earned relationship with one particular god. They form a trial-based progression that is separate from divine ancestry, bloodline, godling fraction, adoption, and rank.

## Progression

| Stage | Requirement | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Follower | Worship and follow the god | Recognized member of the faith |
| Blessed | A heroic follower completes two total trials for the god | The god's followers sense the divine favor; the character gains the Advanced simple template |
| Champion | Complete three total trials and the god's Champion office is vacant | Become the god's unique Champion and a leader among the god's followers; gain two godly powers tied to that god's domains |

Each god can have only one Champion. Completing a third trial does not remove or replace a serving Champion; the office must already be vacant.

Champion powers are selected using the same framework as godly bloodline powers. Their source is the Champion office rather than ancestry, so both powers must belong to the domains of the Champion's god. Because each godly power also contributes to Mythic Tier and Mythic Power under CR-26, the two Champion powers participate in those calculations normally.

The progression is cumulative: a Champion retains the recognition and Advanced simple template gained as a Blessed unless a later ruling changes that interpretation.

## Known campaign examples

- **Amparo Decoris Ignatius** is Blessed of Hestia, which establishes that they completed two Hestian trials even though the individual trials are not yet recorded. Eris has separately declared Tradegulf to be Amparo's first trial toward becoming Eris's future Champion.
- **Binky** is Blessed of Ena, establishing two completed trials for Ena; their details are not yet recorded.
- **Maarin** is Persephone's Champion. She therefore completed three Persephone trials, holds Persephone's unique Champion office, leads among Persephone's followers, and has two Persephone-domain Champion powers. The identities of those two powers are not yet recorded.
