# Divine Progression Engine

Divine Progression covers Hermes's gifts, demigod abilities, custom obedience progression, legendary-item development, apotheosis, and the costs imposed by Fatal Flaws.

## Mythic progression formula

Let `G` be unlocked godly powers, `P` be unlocked mythic path powers, and `F` be Mythic Feats.

- `Mythic Tier = G + floor(P / 2)`
- `Mythic Power points = 2G + 2P + F`

Every godly power therefore raises tier and supplies 2 Mythic Power points. Path powers raise tier in complete pairs, but each one supplies 2 Mythic Power points immediately. Every Mythic Feat supplies 1 Mythic Power point regardless of whether it was purchased with Boons or gained through another approved source.

## Divine trial progression

Blessed and Champion are god-specific stages earned through divine trials. They do not by themselves change a character's ancestry, bloodline, godling fraction, adoption, or divine rank.

| Stage | Requirement | Mechanical and social effect |
|---|---|---|
| Follower | Worships and follows the god | Member of that god's faith |
| Blessed | Heroic follower who completes two total trials for that god | Other followers sense the god's favor; gain the Advanced simple template |
| Champion | Complete three total trials for that god and enter a vacant Champion office | Unique leader among the god's followers; gain two additional godly powers drawn from that god's domains |

Each god has only one Champion. Three completed trials make a follower eligible, but cannot displace an existing Champion. Champion godly powers follow the same selection rules as bloodline godly powers except that the Champion's patron domains, rather than the character's ancestry, constrain the choices. The progression is cumulative unless the Game Master rules otherwise.

This separation permits mixed divine relationships. A character can carry one god's bloodline, be Blessed by that god or another, and pursue a third god's vacant Champion office, subject to the gods' reactions and the campaign narrative.

## Current divine gifts and divine-linked assets

- Seven-Pipped Gem: divine ability from Hermes progression.
- Hermes's Boots of Speed: divine gift supporting action economy and spell supremacy.
- Eyebrow Piercing of Confidence: legendary item and mental-stat multiplier.
- Custom Hermes obedience and divine-power system.

## Design doctrine

Choose divine abilities that reinforce Probability, Magical Supremacy, Influence, or Infrastructure. Avoid duplicating effects already supplied by artifacts unless the redundancy protects against suppression, theft, or action-economy conflicts.
