# Pathfinder Campaign Folder: Content-Level Review

## Scope and overlap

The requested `Pathfinder/Campaign Setting` path was not present. The available collection is `C:\Users\coutu\Documents\Codex\Documents\Pathfinder\Campaign`, which was treated as the intended source. Its 70 PDFs contain 5,315 pages, including 5,236 text-bearing pages and 21,730,701 extracted characters.

SHA-256 comparison found 49 files that are exact duplicates of books already reviewed in `Chronicles & Campaign Setting`. The remaining 21 files received the substantive gap review. The local page text remains in ignored temporary storage; this repository retains metadata, concise summaries, citations, and campaign analysis.

Campaign rules and Zatera lore remain authoritative. Golarion organizations, divine ranks, prestige systems, and setting assumptions are reference material unless the GM adopts them.

## Highest-value findings

### 1. The Godclaw mystery describes a likely enemy oracle package

*Path of the Hellknight* gives Order of the Godclaw oracles a mystery whose Might of the Godclaw revelation grants Deific Obedience and permits substituting boons among several Godclaw deities. Other revelations provide instant heavy armor, coercive commands, rerolls against disabling effects, and defensive benefits.

This does not explain why the order pursued Demidius, but it establishes a published divine-flexibility package their high-level oracles may possess. Prepare for layered divine defenses, heavy armor appearing as an immediate action, suggestion effects, and defensive rerolls.

**Source:** *Path of the Hellknight*, PDF pp. 28-29 / printed pp. 26-27.

### 2. Scrutinize Spell supports counterspell identification

After identifying a target's casting class, Scrutinize Spell adds the user's Wisdom or Charisma modifier to later Spellcraft checks to identify spells from that class list. It does not improve dispel or counterspell rolls directly, but reliable identification determines whether the correct response is available.

**Source:** *Path of the Hellknight*, PDF p. 41 / printed p. 39.

**Disposition:** A- for Demidius if identification failures are occurring; otherwise secondary to direct caster-level improvements.

### 3. Counter-divination rooms are strong Sounon infrastructure

Lead-lined rooms block divinations when seamlessly closed, but become airtight. Secrecy runes block sound and incoming divinations up to 3rd, 5th, or 7th level even while portals are open. Published downtime augmentations give costs and construction times for both approaches.

A superior rune-inscribed planning room is the leading model for a Sounon war room or Key of Daedalus staging chamber. Lead is cheaper but creates ventilation and closed-door constraints. Neither should be assumed to defeat artifacts, deities, mythic exceptions, or higher-level magic.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Intrigue*, PDF p. 59 / printed p. 57.

### 4. Masked personas fit Alley, Roy, and covert crew operations

Any class can establish a consistent masked persona. After operating long enough to gain a level, the persona can be treated as a separate identity for several divinations and for social or legal reputation. The disguise is compromised when too many people connect it to the character.

Convincing Persona improves Bluff and Disguise and prevents ordinary public compromise short of a major unmasking. Masked Symbol can represent Faith, Fear, Hope, Leadership, Mystery, the People, or Trust. Alley is the best operational lead; Roy's exceptional lying makes him a natural supporting identity specialist.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Intrigue*, PDF pp. 14-15 / printed pp. 12-13.

### 5. Assumed likeness is a compact bard infiltration spell

This bard 1 spell makes each target perceive the caster as someone associated with a chosen emotion. It grants disguise support and can improve Bluff, Diplomacy, Intimidate, or Sleight of Hand depending on the emotion. Because each target may see a different person, the caster must improvise carefully.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Intrigue*, PDF p. 60 / printed p. 58.

**Disposition:** A for Roy's spell-access audit; it complements rather than replaces a persistent masked persona.

### 6. Underwater spell behavior needs an operations card

*Aquatic Adventures* clarifies that ordinary cloud spells do not work underwater, many weather spells become inappropriate, cold can become half cold and half piercing, ordinary fire spells require a caster-level check and create steam on success, invisible creatures still displace visible water, and verbal casting consumes breath.

These rules matter to both galleons, especially for boarding, salvage, submerged ruins, and rescue. Record which subset the GM uses before an underwater encounter.

**Source:** *Aquatic Adventures*, PDF pp. 48-49 / printed pp. 46-47.

### 7. Invisibility bubble solves underwater displacement

The invisibility bubble line conceals both a target and the water it displaces, solving a limitation of ordinary invisibility underwater. The bard list makes the base and giant versions candidates for Roy; wizard access makes the line potentially available through Demidius's CR-09 ruling.

**Source:** *Aquatic Adventures*, PDF p. 62 / printed p. 60.

### 8. Pressure magic belongs in fleet emergency stores

Pressure adaptation reduces the saves needed to adjust to a new depth, while stabilize pressure adjusts the target immediately to its current pressure. Neutral buoyancy and free-swim effects further reduce underwater movement hazards. These are crew-survival tools, not primary level-17 combat selections.

**Source:** *Aquatic Adventures*, PDF pp. 62-63 / printed pp. 60-61.

### 9. Aquachymist is useful design research for Binky, not a free addition

The aquachymist modifies bombs so they work underwater and can be weighted to rise or sink. Binky already uses the gun chemist archetype, so archetype compatibility and replaced class features must be checked before borrowing any mechanics.

**Source:** *Aquatic Adventures*, PDF p. 52 / printed p. 50.

### 10. Planefarer's band is a compact elemental survival item

The ring can attune to an Elemental Plane and provides flight, earth traversal, fire resistance and lava walking, or water breathing and a swim speed. Its scope is limited to elemental environments; it does not solve Tartarus operations and competes for a ring slot.

**Source:** *Planes of Power* (PZO9295), PDF p. 11 / printed p. 9.

### 11. Janni's jaunt is a restricted planar transport option

Janni's jaunt transports only willing creatures and only among the Material, Astral, and Elemental Planes. The Key of Daedalus is superior for visited destinations, but this spell is a useful fallback when its narrower destination list fits.

**Source:** *Planes of Power* (PZO9295), PDF p. 11 / printed p. 9.

### 12. Published deity and demigod counts are reference, not campaign law

*Inner Sea Faiths* presents a Golarion convention: full deities normally grant five domains and six subdomains, while demigods grant four and four. It also permits specially granted subdomains without granting their associated domains.

Zatera's divine ranks, Boons, bloodlines, Blessed status, and Champion office are independent campaign systems. Use the counts only as a balancing reference when a deity's portfolio is missing, never to overwrite established gods.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Faiths* (PZO9290), PDF p. 5 / printed p. 3.
