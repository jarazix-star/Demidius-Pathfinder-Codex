# Pathfinder Chronicles & Campaign Setting: Content-Level Review

## Scope and method

This pass indexed all 76 PDFs in `Pathfinder/Chronicles & Campaign Setting` at page level: 5,705 PDF pages, 5,630 pages with extractable text, and 23,946,060 extracted characters. Searches were run across the entire corpus, then the strongest passages were read in context. The full extracted text remains in ignored local research storage; this repository retains only bibliographic metadata, citations, summaries, and campaign analysis.

This collection mixes Pathfinder First Edition material with older Pathfinder Chronicles/3.5-era books. Options from older books require conversion. Golarion lore and organization rules are inspiration, not Zatera canon. Nothing below supersedes the campaign's established Boon, Blessed, Champion, divine-rank, mythic-tier, ship, or epic rules.

## Highest-value findings

### 1. A published framework for custom divine obediences and boons

*Inner Sea Gods* explicitly supports adapting Deific Obedience to lesser or custom deities. It recommends comparing domains and borrowing same-level boons from core deities, including mixing choices from multiple deities or divine prestige-class tracks when appropriate. This is the best rules-development framework in the collection for creating balanced Hermes, Aphrodite, Hestia, Persephone, Ena, and other Zateran deity packages.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Gods* (PZO9267), PDF p. 175 / printed p. 174.

**Campaign boundary:** these published obediences and boons are a separate subsystem. They do not replace divine trials, campaign Boons used as currency, the Blessed template, the unique office of Champion, or bloodline godly powers.

### 2. Evangelist is the strongest divine prestige-class lead for Demidius

Evangelist requires Deific Obedience and advances the abilities of an aligned base class at every prestige-class level after the first, while retaining the prestige class's own chassis. It also accelerates deity boons at prestige levels 3, 6, and 9 and culminates in Spiritual Form. If epic/post-20 advancement permits prestige classes and a custom Hermes obedience is approved, this is the cleanest published way to deepen Demidius's Hermes identity without abandoning his main class engine.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Gods* (PZO9267), PDF pp. 199–200 / printed pp. 198–199.

**Access questions:** epic progression, oracle feature advancement, custom Hermes obedience, alignment, and interaction with campaign divine offices all require GM approval.

### 3. Exalted offers full divine spell progression and a domain expansion

Exalted advances divine spellcasting at all ten levels, grants accelerated divine boons, and at level 5 adds a selected domain from the deity's portfolio. For a divine caster, the domain's spells can become once-per-day spell-like abilities when the character can cast divine spells of that level. Its level-10 miracle is powerful, but losing one level of oracle class features may matter more than maintaining spell slots.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Gods* (PZO9267), PDF pp. 201–202 / printed pp. 200–201.

**Disposition:** secondary to Evangelist for Demidius; potentially excellent for a dedicated divine follower.

### 4. Mythic trials and mythic power need not be the same event

*Mythic Realms* states that claiming mythic power and completing a mythic trial are not necessarily identical and emphasizes story-appropriate awards. This supports the campaign's existing separation between divine trials, Boon currency, godly powers, mythic path powers, and mythic tier. A divine trial should not automatically alter mythic tier unless the GM separately awards a qualifying power.

**Source:** *Mythic Realms* (PZO9262), PDF p. 7.

### 5. The galleon and captain-derived sailing rules directly affect the Dawnrunner

*Ships of the Inner Sea* describes galleons as durable, heavily armed merchant or warships with high forecastles and sterncastles. It also states that its ship statistics assume the most skilled sailor—usually the captain—makes required sailing checks and that the captain's statistics are already built into listed initiative, AC, and saving throws.

**Source:** *Ships of the Inner Sea* (PZO9274), PDF pp. 5 and 7 / printed pp. 3 and 5.

**Action:** recalculate the Dawnrunner's derived statistics after Demidius's current Profession (sailor) modifier and staffing level are supplied. The book validates the importance of that update but does not override the campaign workbook formulas.

The same book summarizes the Skull & Shackles plunder abstraction: roughly one point of plunder represents 1,000 gp and one ton of valuable cargo. It is a useful bookkeeping option for the two galleons, not an adopted campaign rule.

### 6. Artifact research should be an explicit campaign procedure

*Artifacts & Legends* recommends deliberate research rather than automatic identification. Artifact abilities are not fully exposed by *identify* or *analyze dweomer*; *legend lore* provides history rather than a complete rules readout. A specialized library can support a week of research followed by a Knowledge check, with library bonuses typically ranging from +2 to +5. Knowledge DCs commonly begin at 15 + artifact caster level, with more information for higher results.

**Source:** *Artifacts & Legends* (PZO9250), PDF pp. 6–7 / printed pp. 4–5.

**Action:** use the new Artifact Research Protocol for the Key of Daedalus, Scepter of Keto, Deck of Many Things, and newly recovered artifacts. Exact DCs and revelations remain GM-controlled.

### 7. Riftwarden is a specialist solution for planar corridor security

The Riftwarden prestige class specializes in abjuration, planar knowledge, and defending against evil outsiders and portals. It is a strong organizational fit for securing the Key of Daedalus's planar routes and any Tartarus operation, but its prerequisites and class costs make it more attractive for a recruited specialist or follower than for Demidius.

**Source:** *Paths of Prestige* (PZO9249), PDF pp. 48–49.

### 8. Spellscar revelations identify useful anti-magic tools

The Spellscar oracle mystery contains several relevant revelations. Magic Penetration improves caster-level checks to dispel or remove magic; Mystic Null improves defenses against spells and later supernatural effects; its Spell Resistance revelation grants scaling spell resistance. Because an oracle normally cannot change mysteries and Demidius uses another mystery, these are research targets only if a legal revelation-access mechanism, retraining ruling, or custom divine reward exists.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Magic* (PZO9237), PDF p. 32 / printed p. 30.

### 9. Iroran Paladin provides a rare defense against forced rerolls and unluck

The Iroran Paladin archetype's Aura of Excellence protects the paladin from effects that force rerolls or require the worse of two rolls and helps nearby allies resist them; Aura of Perfection later improves protection from effects that force a second result. This is unusually well matched to the campaign's luck/unluck threats.

**Source:** *Inner Sea Combat* (PZO9268), PDF p. 41 / printed p. 39.

**Disposition:** strong follower, cohort, or allied-paladin concept; not a Demidius option without major rebuilding.

### 10. Fame and Prestige systems are useful infrastructure models

The Pathfinder Society Field Guide's Fame/Prestige and vanity rules supply portable models for rewards, property, organizations, ships, temples, islands, and retainers. Its ship vanity makes a vessel self-sustaining rather than inherently profitable and provides a Profession (sailor) benefit. Faction Guide and Inner Sea Magic add models for purchasing services, operating schools, research assistance, spell transcription, and maintaining benefits remotely.

**Sources:** *Pathfinder Society Field Guide* (PZO9235), PDF pp. 15 and 62–64; *Faction Guide* (PZO9221), PDF p. 21; *Inner Sea Magic* (PZO9237), PDF pp. 21 and 29.

**Disposition:** adapt as an optional Sounon/Dawnrunner/Matcha Frappuccino organization engine only if the GM wants a quantified influence economy. Do not introduce Prestige Points into play by assumption.

### 11. Isles of the Shackles is an encounter-and-location toolbox

*Isles of the Shackles* offers island gazetteers, hidden harbors, shipwrecks, pirate crews, draugr, and ghost-ship material. These are useful templates for populating the Isles of Berres, especially isolated islands and cursed naval encounters. Their Golarion names, history, factions, and statistics are not automatically true in Zatera.

**Source:** *Isles of the Shackles* (PZO9244), especially PDF pp. 58 and 64–65.

### 12. Legacy material needs conversion labels

The older *Campaign Setting* includes options such as Storm-Lashed and an older Weather domain. They use 3.5-era assumptions and setting prerequisites. They may inspire weatherproof sailors or divine weather packages, but they require conversion and should never be placed directly on a PF1 character sheet.

**Source:** *Campaign Setting* (PZO1111), PDF pp. 135 and 161.

## Negative and limiting findings

- The exact terms **Diverse Obedience** and **Divine Interference** did not appear in this 76-book corpus, so this library does not independently verify those options.
- Winter Witch is thematically relevant to Aristea's cold magic but is written for witch access; Aristea was a wizard, so it is not a clean legal option.
- Music of the Spheres can support healing and energy resistance, but it requires concentration and affects creatures in its emanation without clean friend/foe selection. It is situational rather than a core recommendation.
- The Pale Maiden luck blade overlaps existing luck infrastructure and is not owned by the party.
- Published classes using the word “champion,” including Sentinel, do not confer or compete with the campaign's unique divine office of Champion.
