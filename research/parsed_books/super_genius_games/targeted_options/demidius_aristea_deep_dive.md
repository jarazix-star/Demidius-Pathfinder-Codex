# Super Genius Games Deep Dive: Demidius and Aristea

## Scope and method

This pass searched the 143-PDF Super Genius Games library for material matching Demidius's control, luck, dispelling, teleportation, leadership, mythic, and legendary-item engines, and Aristea's damage, ray/sneak-attack, and accelerated-crafting engines. Thirteen previously unprocessed books (61 PDF pages) were selected for content-level extraction. Every rules page was text-extracted and visually checked against a rendered page image.

Page references below are PDF page numbers. Summaries are paraphrases; consult the cited page for operative wording. “Candidate” means mechanically interesting, not automatically campaign-legal.

## Executive findings

### Demidius: strongest candidates

1. **Mythic Restoration** — restores a daily nonmythic ability by spending mythic power. This could recycle a high-impact oracle, godling, artifact, or other limited-use feature, subject to the feature being nonmythic. *6 Mythic Feats*, PDF p. 2.
2. **Mythic Focus** — three restricted extra mythic-power uses for one named mythic expenditure. Excellent if Demidius has a repeatable, build-defining mythic activation. *6 Mythic Feats*, PDF p. 2.
3. **Augment Teleportation** — carries extra material, reaches willing passengers within 15 feet, rearranges arrivals, and controls falling speed. This is the cleanest improvement to Demidius's planar-logistics role. *6 Teleportation Spell Feats*, PDF p. 2.
4. **Slow Teleport + Unexpected Arrival** — creates a protected one-round transit interval, allows destination scrying, and ultimately permits an instantaneous spell to resolve at the destination just before arrival. Powerful, but feat-intensive. *6 Teleportation Spell Feats*, PDF pp. 2–3.
5. **Master of Speed** — redistributes the party's rolled initiative results. This is exceptional encounter sequencing for a battlefield controller. *5 Haste/Slow Feats*, PDF pp. 2–3.
6. **Overlord of Speed** — converts a failed save against Demidius's effect into a second save or slow, while granting an ally haste or blessing of fervor for the same duration. *5 Haste/Slow Feats*, PDF p. 3.
7. **Antischool Specialization** — makes antimagic or dispelling affect only one school. This can sharply reduce friendly-fire and target a hostile magical engine, but requires the listed spells and Spell Focus (abjuration). *6 Antimagic Field Feats*, PDF p. 2.
8. **Augment Healing** — rolls healing dice twice and keeps the better result. It is reliable, but needs Spell Focus (conjuration) and only applies to conjuration (healing) spells. *10 Subschool Augmentation Feats*, PDF p. 3.
9. **Warning** — a legendary item can buy surprise-round action economy with legendary power. This is a strong candidate for Demidius's legendary item if the campaign permits the new legendary-item ability. *6 Powers for Legendary Weapons*, PDF p. 2.
10. **Allies and Associates** — replaces conventional Leadership with safe-location research, spellcasting, and item-crafting support. It is strategically excellent but replaces, rather than supplements, the normal feat. *Two Options for Leadership*, PDF p. 2.

### Aristea: strongest candidates

1. **Caustic Magic** — after Aristea deals acid damage, a swift-action Fortitude save can make the target count as flanked by her for one round. If her sneak-attack rays qualify under the campaign ruling, this is the most direct engine piece in the pass. It is a wizard arcane discovery. *New Arcane Discoveries*, PDF p. 2.
2. **Fierce Caster** — trades spell attack accuracy for substantial hit-point damage on spells and spell-like abilities requiring attack rolls. This directly matches a ray attacker, but scales to a severe attack penalty and is a wizard discovery. *New Arcane Discoveries*, PDF p. 3.
3. **Spellbane** — damaging a target with a spell can make that damage count as continuous damage for its concentration checks until Aristea's next turn. Strong pressure after a damaging ray. *New Arcane Discoveries*, PDF p. 7.
4. **Targeted Missiles** — turns magic missiles into ranged touch attacks, permitting attacks against objects and creatures with full concealment. This loses automatic accuracy and requires Dexterity 13, Deadly Aim, BAB +1, and magic missile. *7 Feats for Magic Missile*, PDF p. 3.
5. **Legendary Missiles** — scalable metamagic increases both missile damage dice and effective spell level. Potentially useful for damage stacking, but slot-intensive. *7 Feats for Magic Missile*, PDF p. 3.
6. **Implosion / Shockwave** — trades 1 damage per die from a fireball-like spell for mass drag or bull-rush control using caster level plus spell level as CMB. Excellent damage-plus-positioning tools. *5 Fireball Feats*, PDF pp. 2–3.
7. **Unsensed Item** — makes an invisible object silent and scentless, improves relevant Bluff/Sleight of Hand checks, and reduces the action cost of feinting with an unsensed weapon. This is useful only if Aristea has an attack routine that benefits from feinting. *4 Invisibility Feats*, PDF p. 2.
8. **Craft Tige Vierge** — front-loads most of a staff's crafting time into a blank shell, then binds its spells in one eight-hour day. This strongly complements the campaign's accelerated crafting, but needs Intelligence 17, caster level 11, Craft Staff, and GM acceptance of the subsystem. *Magic Staff Emporium*, PDF pp. 3–4.
9. **Staff of Winter (greater)** — includes polar ray plus broad cold control, and can recharge twice as fast under its environmental condition. This is the best printed staff for a ray specialist among the ten. *Magic Staff Emporium*, PDF pp. 10–11.
10. **Arcane Vengeance** — casts a prepared touch spell as an attack of opportunity once per day. It is potent only if Aristea is a wizard with a suitable touch-spell package. *New Arcane Discoveries*, PDF p. 2.

## Compatibility and ruling queue

1. **Aristea's class and access method:** confirm whether she is a wizard, has arcane discoveries, or can buy them through a boon. Until then, all discovery ratings are conditional.
2. **Aristea's exact ray ruling:** determine whether making magic missile a ranged touch attack through Targeted Missiles allows her special ray/sneak-attack interaction. The feat does not itself call the spell a ray.
3. **Godling prerequisite:** decide whether Demidius's campaign godling bloodline counts as “Godling” for these feats or whether a level in an SGG godling class is required.
4. **Legendary item scope:** verify that Demidius's legendary item may select these legendary-item powers even when it is not a weapon.
5. **Antischool Specialization:** specify whether the campaign interprets school-limited dispelling as ignoring all nonselected-school effects during both targeted and area dispels.
6. **Crafting acceleration:** decide how the campaign's accelerated crafting multiplier interacts with the 75% sunk cost and one-day finishing stage of a tige vierge.
7. **Leadership replacement:** Allies and Associates should replace the normal cohort/followers package; do not add its benefits for free.
8. **Third-party balance review:** Mythic Empowerment, Mythic Focus, Mythic Restoration, Unexpected Arrival, Focused Invisibility, and Caustic Magic deserve explicit GM approval before build commitment.

## Recommended acquisition order

### Demidius

1. Check **Mythic Restoration**, **Mythic Focus**, and **Mythic Empowerment** against his current mythic sheet.
2. If feat access is available, take **Augment Teleportation** before considering the Slow Teleport chain.
3. Check **Master of Speed** and then **Overlord of Speed** against his spell list and feat economy.
4. Treat **Antischool Specialization** as a dispel-engine sidegrade, not an automatic pick, until the school-targeting ruling is settled.
5. Consider **Warning** as a legendary-item power and **Staff of Scrying** as infrastructure rather than personal-slot purchases.

### Aristea

1. Confirm class, feats, primary damage types, and the exact ray/sneak-attack ruling.
2. If arcane discoveries are legal, test **Caustic Magic**, **Fierce Caster**, **Spellbane**, and **Lingering Arcana** first.
3. If magic missile is part of her routine, compare **Targeted Missiles** and **Legendary Missiles** against her existing ray spells; do not assume the former counts as a ray.
4. If fire area spells are common, test **Implosion** and **Shockwave** before the more situational fireball feats.
5. For crafting, price a **tige vierge** custom staff and compare it with a greater **Staff of Winter** under the campaign's time multiplier.
