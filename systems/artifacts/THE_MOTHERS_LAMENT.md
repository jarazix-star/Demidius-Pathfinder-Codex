# The Mother's Lament

*The Mother's Lament* is a famous book banned across the world. It recounts the suffering of three Mothers of Monsters—Keto, Echidna, and Gaia—and presents the gods, especially the sea gods, as perpetrators or enablers of the tragedies inflicted on the mothers and their children.

The book's claims are recorded here as the artifact's narrative. Their presence in the text does not by itself establish that every detail is an objective or uncontested historical account.

## Content warning

The text includes sexual violence, divine abuse, mutilation or transformation, killing, enslavement, and violence against children and descendants.

## The lament of Keto

The book describes Keto and her many children as victims of assault, curses, and persecution by the gods. Its examples include:

- Scylla, cursed by Amphitrite and transformed into a monster.
- Medusa, raped by Poseidon and then cursed by Athena.

## The lament of Echidna

The book identifies Echidna as Keto's daughter and describes her being forced to watch the gods use Hercules's Labors to torment and destroy her children:

- Hercules killed the Nemean Lion and wore its skin.
- He used that skin as protection while killing the Hydra.
- During the taking of Geryon's cattle, he killed Geryon and Orthros, another child of Echidna.
- He killed Ladon with poisoned arrows while taking the golden apples.
- He fought Cerberus while wearing the Nemean Lion's skin, subdued Cerberus, and dragged it from the Underworld for mortal captivity until Hades recovered it.

## The lament of Gaia

The book says Poseidon sexually assaulted Gaia while she slept, resulting in the birth of Charybdis, a powerful sea nymph or goddess. Poseidon then used Charybdis to expand his domain by breaking continents and pulling islands beneath the sea. Zeus punished Charybdis by transforming her into the great sea monster imprisoned beneath a whirlpool in the Sea of Monsters. Poseidon did not intervene, allowing his daughter to bear the consequences of his policy.

## Suppression by the Olympian Council

After the fall of the Titans, the Olympian Council banned the book. The stated reasons were fear that its circulation would reduce worship of the Big Three—Hades, Zeus, and Poseidon—and increase worship of Keto, Echidna, and Gaia.

The ban makes the book a religious and political threat even before any magical ability is considered. Possession, copying, translation, public reading, or use as propaganda may attract divine, institutional, or legal retaliation.
