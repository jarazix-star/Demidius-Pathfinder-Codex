# Blood of the Lernaean Hydra

This poison is blood taken from the **Lernaean Hydra**, identified in the campaign record as the true Hydra of Greek mythology. The supplied history describes it as the blood in which Hercules dipped his arrows and as the poison that ultimately killed him after it contaminated another person's blood.

## Quantity record

- The supplied account identifies a stock of **ten vials**.
- Demidius currently possesses **two doses**.
- It is not yet established whether one vial equals one dose, who holds the remaining vials, or whether the ten-vial figure describes the original or current total stock.

## Confirmed campaign mechanics

| Field | Supplied rule |
|---|---|
| Save DC | 40 |
| First round | Save versus death; a creature that passes takes 1d6 Constitution damage |
| Ongoing damage | 2d6 Constitution damage per round |
| Duration | 10 rounds |
| Cure condition | 3 consecutive successful saves |

These mechanics are recorded as campaign-specific rules. They have not been normalized to the standard Pathfinder poison format.

## Interpretation questions

- Which saving throw is used, although Fortitude is typical for poison.
- Whether a failed first-round save causes immediate death without Constitution damage.
- Whether the first-round death check is included in the ten-round duration.
- Whether an ongoing successful save prevents that round's Constitution damage.
- The delivery method: injury, contact, ingested, blood contact, or some combination.
- The exposure interval and whether multiple doses stack or reset the duration.
- Whether poison immunity, death-effect immunity, mythic defenses, divine defenses, or *neutralize poison* apply normally.
- Whether one vial contains one dose.

## Operational doctrine

Because accidental exposure may be immediately fatal, store each dose in a separately sealed, labeled, warded container. Track custody and dose count after every transfer. Do not coat a weapon or open a vial without a declared target, recovery plan, contamination plan, and confirmation of how the campaign adjudicates immunities and antidotes.

This doctrine is a safety recommendation, not an additional campaign rule.
